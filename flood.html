Flood Stage Classifications

Understanding flood severity levels and appropriate response actions

Minor Flooding

Action Stage

Minimal or no property damage, but possibly some public threat or inconvenience. Some overbank flooding of low-lying areas.
Actions: Monitor conditions, prepare supplies, avoid flooded roads
Moderate Flooding

Minor Flood Stage

Some inundation of structures and roads near streams. Evacuations of people and transfer of property to higher elevations may be necessary.
Actions: Prepare to evacuate, move valuables to higher floors, secure property
Major Flooding

Moderate Flood Stage

Extensive inundation and property damage. Large-scale evacuations and emergency services response required.
Actions: Evacuate immediately if ordered, avoid all flooded areas, emergency shelter
Record Flooding

Major Flood Stage

Rare flooding with significant threat to life and property. Catastrophic damage expected. Emergency response fully activated.
Actions: Follow all evacuation orders, seek highest ground, emergency services only

Critical Safety Warning
Never attempt to drive through flooded roads. Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down, and 12 inches can carry away a vehicle. Turn around, don't drown. Find an alternate route or wait for waters to recede.
