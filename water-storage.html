Water Storage Requirements

Family Size Calculation

Calculate total water needs based on household members, including pets and extended family who may shelter with you.

Example: Family of 4
4 people × 1 gallon × 3 days = 12 gallons minimum

Extended Duration

Consider scenarios requiring longer than 72 hours. FEMA recommends 2-week supply for comprehensive preparedness.

Extended: Family of 4, 14 days
4 people × 1 gallon × 14 days = 56 gallons total

Storage Considerations

Account for multiple uses: drinking, cooking, hygiene, and medical needs. Increase by 50% for hot climates or medical conditions.

Hot climate adjustment
Base requirement × 1.5 = 18 gallons (3-day)

Quality Standards

Use food-grade containers only. Avoid containers that previously held chemicals, milk, or fruit juices.

Container Standards
FDA food-grade, BPA-free, UV-resistant materials
Critical Safety Note
Never assume tap water will be available during emergencies. Municipal water systems can fail due to power outages, contamination, or infrastructure damage. Having your own water supply is the only way to guarantee access to safe drinking water.

Installation & Setup Guide

1

Location Selection

Choose a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight. Avoid areas near chemicals, gasoline, or other toxins. Ensure accessibility for rotation and emergency access.

2

Container Preparation

Clean containers with mild bleach solution (1 tsp per quart). Rinse thoroughly with clean water. Allow to air dry completely before filling.

3

Water Treatment

If using tap water, add 8 drops of liquid bleach per gallon (16 drops if water is cloudy). Let stand 30 minutes before sealing containers.

4

Proper Sealing

Fill containers to within 1 inch of top to allow for expansion. Seal tightly and wipe exterior dry. Label with date and contents.

5

Storage Organization

Store off concrete floors using pallets or shelving. Maintain consistent temperature. Keep inventory log for rotation schedule.

6

Access Planning

Ensure clear pathways to water storage. Have manual pumps and siphons available. Keep containers accessible even in dark conditions.

Installation Safety
Large water containers are extremely heavy when full (55-gallon barrel weighs 460+ pounds). Ensure proper structural support and use appropriate equipment for moving. Never attempt to move full containers manually.

Maintenance Schedule

Monthly

Visual Inspection

Check for leaks, damage, or contamination. Verify seals are tight and containers show no signs of degradation. Ensure storage area remains cool and dry.

6 Months

Water Rotation

Replace stored water with fresh supply. Use old water for non-emergency purposes like watering plants. Clean and inspect containers during rotation.

Annual

Deep System Check

Test all pumps, spigots, and distribution equipment. Replace any degraded components. Update inventory logs and expiration dates. Verify purification supplies are current.

5 Years

Container Replacement

Replace plastic containers after 5 years of service. UV degradation and plastic fatigue can compromise integrity. Upgrade to newer standards if available.

Quality Testing

Visual Assessment

Check for cloudiness, color changes, or visible particles. Water should be crystal clear with no unusual odor.

Test Kit Usage

Use water testing strips to check pH, chlorine levels, and bacterial contamination. Test annually or if quality is suspected.

Purification Backup

When in doubt, always purify before consumption. Boiling is most reliable, followed by filtration and chemical treatment.
